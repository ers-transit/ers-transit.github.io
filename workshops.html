Exo-Webb: Pre-Launch ERS Hackathon

We are planning a small training and development workshop for transiting exoplanet science with JWST, to be held July 13-15, 2020. The meeting is currently planned to be held at the University of Colorado in Boulder, CO, USA, with a satellite meeting at MPIA Heidelberg and options for remote participation. In light of the continued spread of coronavirus, there is a significant possibility this workshop will be held entirely remotely, but for now we will continue to make plans to allow for both in-person and remote participation.

With an emphasis on learning and inclusive engagement with preparing for JWST, this workshop will center on simulated data from the JWST Transiting Exoplanet Community Early Release Science program, and it will include brief science talks on exoplanet atmospheres, coding tutorials, and collaborative development of tools and recipes. Everyone is welcome to apply, whether or not you have a previous association with the ERS team.

The application to participate is now open. April 1 is the due date for full consideration, and we plan for decisions to be sent out by April 15.

Previous (Related) Workshops

  • The Enabling Transiting Exoplanet Observations with JWST (2017) workshop was held July 10-12, 2017 at STScI. Conversations at this meeting contributed to the organization and mobilization of our large community team to draft the Early Release Science proposal, as summarized in Bean et al. (2018).
  • The Enabling Transiting Exoplanet Science with JWST (2015) workshop was held November 16-18, 2015 at STScI. Conversations at this meeting contributed to the initial identification appropriate targets for the Early Release Science program, as summarized in Stevenson et al. (2016).

Future Workshops

We plan to hold additional data challenge workshops through 2021 and beyond, after JWST's launch, both (1) to finalize instrument-specific analysis and interpretation tools and (2) to compare analyses of the actual Early Release Science observations.
